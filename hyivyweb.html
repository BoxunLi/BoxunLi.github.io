HYIVY WEBSITE DESIGN

May - August 2020

INTRO

Hyivy Health is a start up aiming to create a pelvic rehabilitation device with trackable data for women who have pelvic cancers and diseases to help alleviate pelvic health symptoms after a devastating diagnosis, treatment or surgery.

ROLE

During my internship at Hyivy Health, I worked as a web developer, UI/UX designer, brand designer, and 3D product designer. This section is focused on the web design for Hyivy’s new website.

GOAL

The goal was to update Hyivy Health’s old website to help improve the browsing experience for users and to better convey the company’s messages and values.

HOMEPAGE

To start, the old website appeared very traditional. It had a very rigid layout and gave off little personality and energy. To improve it, I adopted more round edges and more borderless elements to make the website look more dynamic, energetic and free as the elements appear to be unbounded and freely floating in the website.

To keep the theme consistent and to build a clearer brand image, I choose to only use 4 colours throughout the entire page, 3 of which are part of the brand logo. I also altered the colours of some of the imagery used with photoshop to better fit the website.

The brighter colours and the consistency in the themes of the imagery also emits the consistent energy of freeness, strength, happiness and hope, which are all Hyivy’s company values.

After the redo the website appears more modern and positive. Its dynamic design catches the viewer’s attention and invites them to read and browse further into the site.

PRODUCT PAGE

I chose a very modern and interactive way to showcase the product, which I also personally designed (more detail

here

). The page uses the same bounaryless style as the homepage. A darker theme is used to make it look more techy, which draws more attention to the white product.

To showcase the functions and components of the product, I added animations of the product slowly opening up as the user scrolls, which increases interactivity between the site and the user as well as catching their attention and delivering that futuristic theme throughout this page.

Since the company isnt goin to have a lot of products in the future. Instead of following the traditional shop page layouts, I decided to make it even easier to explore by enlargening the product icons and sorting them in catagories instead of just having a grid of products. The slider on top also helps customers get where they want to get faster.
